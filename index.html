Connected (2020)

Connected is a mobile app aiming to link & strengthen daily connections between family members who are separated by the pandemic or other realistic factors. With spatial distance narrowed by human-centred design, emotional needs can be better conveyed, so that family could serve as a gentle net to stretch and embrace its members who are far away facing the epidemic alone in this hard year.

UX / UI:  Hong Yang, Celine, Corlyn, Kao YiYong
Website:  Hong Yang

Butterfly love (2021)

Butterfly-love, or called Shen Yuan Ji Shui , a gourmet restaurant mainly based in local ingredients and food culture in Tainan, is now developing an online ordering system. With increasing attention paid to precision health, a pie chart is laid on the prominent position to record the intake of 6 kinds of main nutrients, so that customers can directly see which kind of utrients is insufficient and choose to add the recommended food within a click.

Multi- Screen Splicing System (2021)

When brainstorming what we can do during the waiting time in the restaurant, an idea of splicing mobiles screens via simply scan a QR code comes up, which can be a new platform to encourage customers to put down phones and communicate more by joining small screens into a larger one to watch animations together.


UX/UI:  Hong Yang, Zhuo Yixuan

AgriTalk (2021)

AgriTalk is a smart agricultural platform developed by Prof. Lin Yiping's IOT team, with the strong support from Quanta Computer and Chunghwa Telecom. AgriTalk builds an organic agricultural system merged with AI to fulfil the goal of automatically precise farming. The RWD design is still in a preliminary stage. Since targets users are mainly farmers and researchers, taken technique limits into consideration, all information will be delivered simply and directly.

V I D E O S

Poem of Winds (2020) is a small documentary commemorating the 10th anniversary of Chinese Mainland students being allowed to come to Taiwan for degrees. Interrupted by the pandemic, the permit has been canceled temporarily or maybe permanently.

We' re generally called  Mainland Students in Taiwan. If someone want a poetic image or any metaphor to better describe us , it can be  the wind, which may derive from Chinese writer Han Han's famous article Winds of the Pacific, or from the memory of unforgettable gale in Hsinchu, or from reflections of ourselves who will eventually leave Taiwan just like a gust of wind. We never see any winds, just like we can't see living individuals under the label of a group. The winds do not have any weight, as we know how slight we are when put into the macroscopic cross-strait landscape. Yet even the lightest winds can make a bit difference. People who move across borders can be precious, since their existence to some extent could connect two societies split by history and power, giving us the opportunity to hear voices from the other side, even the voices that we disapprove of.
